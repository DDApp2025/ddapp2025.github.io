Why We Exist

The Problem We Solve

The Problem

Traditional aesthetics is built on overhead.

Licensed physicians, NPs, and PAs entering aesthetic medicine face a structural choice. Practice independently and you carry every fixed cost — clinical real estate, front-desk staff, EHR subscription, supplier contracts, lead generation. Or accept a clinic-based employment role where the employer captures most of the value created by your clinical work. The operating model rewards the asset, not the license. Traditional aesthetic employment is built around the clinic — its lease, its overhead, its patient pipeline — not around the provider whose license makes the practice billable in the first place.

Our Solution

Replace the clinic asset with clinical infrastructure.

Aesthetics To Go is a mobile clinical operating system built around the licensed provider, not the building. Under the Regional Lead Provider agreement, providers receive the full clinical infrastructure traditionally reserved for the clinic owner — mobile EHR, smart scheduling, credentialed lead flow, integrated payments, wholesale supply access, and clinical governance. The fixed overhead of running a practice — lease, staffing, marketing — is absorbed by the operating system during the launch period. Providers retain clinical autonomy, set their own schedule, and operate as 1099 independent contractors with their own malpractice and supervisory frameworks. The clinic asset disappears. The clinical practice scales with you.

Our Clinical Standards

These apply to every provider in the network.
1

Licensed Providers Only

Every provider on Aesthetics To Go holds an active, unrestricted license as a physician (MD/DO), nurse practitioner, or physician assistant. No exceptions. No estheticians performing injections. No unlicensed personnel. Every license is verified during our credentialing process.

2

Malpractice Insurance Required

All providers must carry their own professional liability and malpractice insurance. Coverage must be active and adequate for the aesthetic procedures they perform. Proof of insurance is verified before any provider can accept appointments.

3

Supervisory Compliance

Providers maintain collaborative or supervisory agreements as required by their state's practice act. The mobile EHR produces clean, exportable SOAP notes and treatment records that can be shared with supervising physicians for chart review.

4

FDA-Approved Products Only

All injectable products used through Aesthetics To Go — neurotoxins and dermal fillers — must be FDA-approved and sourced from authorized distributors. Proper cold-chain storage and handling protocols are required. No counterfeit or gray-market products.

5

HIPAA-Compliant Documentation

Every treatment is documented with structured SOAP notes, digital consents, injection site mapping, unit and volume counts, lot numbers and expiration dates, and before-and-after photos. All data is encrypted and stored in a HIPAA-compliant environment.

6

Sterile Technique & Emergency Protocols

Providers follow the same sterile technique and infection control protocols used in medical offices. Emergency supplies and protocols for adverse reactions — including hyaluronidase for filler complications — are required in every mobile clinical kit.

How is this different from a clinic?

Aesthetics To Go is a mobile clinical operating system, not a clinic. Providers come to patients in their homes or offices, with full clinical infrastructure traveling with them.

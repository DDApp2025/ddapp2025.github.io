The Science

How PRP Hair Restoration Works

Your body's own healing power.
1

Blood Draw

A small blood sample is drawn from your arm — similar to a routine lab draw. Quick and minimally uncomfortable.

2

Centrifuge Processing

Your blood is spun in a portable centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelet-rich plasma containing your growth factors.

3

Scalp Injection

The concentrated PRP is injected into targeted areas of thinning using a fine-gauge needle. Numbing can be applied for comfort.

4

Follicle Activation

Growth factors stimulate blood vessel formation, increase follicle size, and shift dormant follicles into active growth phase over 3–6 months.

Is PRP Right for You?

Ideal Candidates for Hair Restoration

Your provider will assess during consultation.

Good Candidates

✓ Early to moderate hair thinning (hair still present but thinner)
✓ Androgenetic alopecia (pattern hair loss) in men or women
✓ Receding hairline with miniaturized but living follicles
✓ Crown thinning or diffuse thinning across the scalp
✓ Post-partum hair thinning
✓ Patients wanting to complement other hair loss treatments

May Need Alternatives

→ Complete baldness in treatment areas (no remaining follicles)
→ Hair loss caused by autoimmune conditions (alopecia areata)
→ Scarring alopecia (follicles permanently destroyed)
→ Active scalp infections or conditions
→ Blood disorders or platelet dysfunction
→ Patients on blood thinners (relative contraindication)

Treatment Details

What to Expect

Before Your Appointment

Simple preparation.

Avoid blood thinners, aspirin, ibuprofen, and alcohol for 48 hours before treatment. Stay well-hydrated in the 24 hours prior. Wash your hair with gentle shampoo the morning of — avoid heavy styling products. Your provider brings all equipment including a portable centrifuge.

During Treatment

About 45 minutes total.

The blood draw takes a few minutes. Centrifuge processing takes 10–15 minutes while you relax. Scalp injections take about 15–20 minutes. Your provider may apply topical numbing before the injections for comfort. Most patients describe mild pressure — not pain.

After Treatment

Minimal restrictions.

Avoid washing your hair for 24 hours. Mild soreness and redness at injection sites is normal and resolves within 1–2 days. Avoid intense exercise and direct sun exposure for 48 hours. You can return to work and normal activities the same day.

Results Timeline

Gradual, natural improvement.

Most patients notice reduced hair shedding within 1–2 months. Visible new growth and thicker existing hair typically appear within 3–6 months. Full results are seen after completing the initial treatment series. Maintenance sessions every 6–12 months sustain results long-term.

Common Questions

Hair Restoration FAQ

Is PRP for hair loss safe?

Yes. PRP uses your own blood, so there's virtually no risk of allergic reaction or rejection. Our licensed providers follow sterile protocols for blood draw, processing, and injection. Mild soreness and redness at injection sites is normal and resolves within 1–2 days.

How many sessions do I need?

Most treatment plans involve 3–4 initial sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart, followed by maintenance treatments every 6–12 months. Your provider will create a personalized plan based on the extent of your thinning and your response to treatment.

Does PRP work for women?

Yes. PRP is effective for both men and women experiencing hair thinning. Female pattern hair loss (diffuse thinning across the top of the scalp) responds particularly well to PRP therapy. Post-partum hair thinning is another common indication.

How much does mobile PRP cost?

Pricing is set by each provider. Because mobile providers have no office overhead, many offer competitive rates. There are no travel fees or surcharges. Multi-session package pricing is typically available. Exact pricing is shown before you book.

Why do PRP at home instead of a clinic?

Privacy is a major factor — many patients prefer to address hair loss discreetly at home rather than in a waiting room. Mobile treatment also eliminates travel time and clinic scheduling constraints. The clinical care is identical; only the setting changes.

Can I combine PRP with other hair loss treatments?

Yes. PRP is often used alongside other treatments like minoxidil or finasteride for enhanced results. Your provider can discuss a comprehensive approach during your consultation and coordinate with your dermatologist or primary care provider if needed.

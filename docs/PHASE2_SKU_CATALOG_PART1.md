## Regulatory Context Summary

On April 15, 2026, the FDA removed twelve peptides (BPC-157, KPV, MOTS-C, TB-500, DSIP/Emideltide, Epitalon, GHRP-2, GHRP-6, Kisspeptin-10, Selank, Semax, Thymosin Alpha-1) from Category 2 of the Bulk Drug Substances Nominated for Use in Compounding list because the nominators withdrew their nominations. Category 2 removal means FDA no longer flags these substances as posing "significant safety risks," but it does **not** equate to approval — each substance still has to clear a Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee (PCAC) review before it can be formally added to the 503A bulks list. The published PCAC schedule: July 23, 2026 for BPC-157, KPV, MOTS-C, TB-500; July 24, 2026 for DSIP, Epitalon, Semax; by end of February 2027 for GHK-Cu, LL-37, Dihexa, PEG-MGF, Melanotan II. Source: [FDA Category 2 list, updated 2026-04-15](https://www.fda.gov/media/94155/download); [Newtropin summary](https://newtropin.com/blog/fda-503a-category-update-april-2026/); [Peptide Schedule summary](https://www.peptideschedule.com/learn/fda-category-2-peptides-removed-2026).

The April 15 action governs the **compounding pharmacy (503A/503B)** pathway only. Oral/sublingual supplement SKUs — the scope of this document — sit under a different regulatory regime entirely: the Dietary Supplement Health and Education Act (DSHEA, 1994). Under DSHEA, ingredients that qualify as dietary ingredients (vitamins, minerals, amino acids, herbs, "dietary substances" used to supplement the diet, or a concentrate/metabolite/constituent of those) can be sold without prescription provided they are labeled as supplements, not marketed with drug claims, and meet cGMP manufacturing standards. Peptide-adjacent products marketed under DSHEA survive FDA scrutiny when they (a) use acetylated/amidated or dipeptide fragments that the manufacturer argues are distinct from the synthetic peptide API on the compounding list, (b) avoid disease-treatment language on the label, and (c) carry COAs and third-party testing.

The practical consequence for ATG Clinical Supplies: the April 2026 reclassification mostly affects the **injectable** peptide market, which is out of scope. What it does affect obliquely is market sentiment — providers are re-engaging with peptide protocols, and a well-positioned supplement-pathway catalog captures that demand without taking on compounding-pharmacy legal exposure. The highest-risk SKUs in this category are **Semax and Selank nasal sprays**, which are neither FDA-approved drugs nor legally compliant dietary supplements in the United States and are typically sold as "research chemicals" with "not for human use" labeling. We are not carrying these ([Innerbody Selank guide](https://www.innerbody.com/selank-peptide); [Semax Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semax)). GHK-Cu oral formulations sit in a softer gray zone — Quicksilver Scientific and others market them as cosmetic/longevity supplements, but PCAC is reviewing GHK-Cu for bulks-list inclusion by February 2027 and a final decision could reshape the category ([Salhab Pharmacy](https://www.salhabpharmacy.com/ghk-cu-fda-legal/)).

## Category A Summary

**Strongest plays.** Integrative Peptides is the anchor brand — BPC-157 PURE (both immediate and delayed release), KPV, and Thymogen Alpha-1 are the four must-stock SKUs and cover the oral peptide segment for ~80% of provider intake. Quicksilver Scientific's liposomal line (NAD+ Gold, Glutathione, Copper GHK, BPC+KPV combo) is the delivery-format differentiator and the premium tier. Designs for Health Whole Body Collagen and Pure Encapsulations Collagen JS together cover the collagen segment cleanly — one powder, one capsule, Types I+III vs. Type II — and both come from practitioner-exclusive brands providers already recognize. Life Extension NAD+ Cell Regenerator plays as the accessible price-anchor. That's 12 SKUs and hits the upper bound of the target range.

**Gray-market adjacent, do not carry.** Semax and Selank nasal sprays. Neither is FDA-approved, neither is a compliant dietary supplement, and both are typically shipped as "research chemicals." Carrying them would compromise the catalog's legitimacy in front of licensed providers and expose ATG to FDA action. Providers who want these peptides are already sourcing from research-chemical vendors; we are not competing in that lane. The Tailor Made Compounding OTC supplement line, which the original research brief asked about, does not appear to have a public DTC supplement channel in 2026 — Tailor Made is primarily an Rx compounding operation ([FDA Tailor Made inspection reference](https://www.fda.gov/media/134965/download)), and there is no published non-Rx OTC peptide line to source from. Exclude from catalog.

**Monitor and flag.** GHK-Cu oral (Quicksilver Copper GHK) sits under PCAC review by February 2027. A favorable PCAC ruling that adds GHK-Cu to the 503A bulks list would not directly affect the oral supplement, but an unfavorable one that extends FDA scrutiny to non-injectable routes could force relabel or pull. Add an internal tracker entry for the February 2027 PCAC outcome. The same monitoring applies to BPC-157 and KPV capsules ahead of the July 23, 2026 PCAC session — the supplement-pathway argument depends on the acetylated/amidated or dipeptide-fragment distinction holding up; if FDA signals that the fragment argument is not accepted, the segment would need rapid repositioning.

**Surprises.** Integrative Peptides' practitioner pricing is more aggressive than peer brands (~40% off retail vs. 25–35% typical). That margin structure makes the brand unusually catalog-friendly and suggests they are actively building practitioner distribution reach. Designs for Health's Progenalen launched only in late 2025 — it's early enough in commercial life that we should expect pricing and packaging updates within 12 months; hold on deep inventory commits. And the Life Extension NAD+ line is priced materially below the liposomal market despite a credible 300 mg NR dose, which creates a real good/better/best pricing ladder on the shelf that benefits conversion.
